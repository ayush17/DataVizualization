This choropleth map visualization displays the global distribution of terrorist attacks on a virtual globe. Users can filter by category like terrorist group, metric like fatalities, and year using the slider to see how patterns change over time. Hovering over countries reveals details, while the "Time Machine" animates the year-by-year changes. Rotate the globe or click on countries to explore regional patterns.

The sunburst charts provide a hierarchical, radial view of terrorist incident data. The left chart shows a breakdown across geographic regions and countries, with ring sizes proportional to incident counts, revealing hotspots. The right chart depicts attack type categories, like bombings and assassinations, with ring sizes indicating prevalence. These compact, nested layouts effectively convey hierarchical distributions and relative contributions, enabling rapid pattern understanding of both the spread and methodologies of terrorist activities.
